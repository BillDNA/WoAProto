Cartographer's Table

Terrain belongs to a hex: click just inside a hex's border to cycle forest → mountain → empty on that hex's side. Forest = +1 attacking out across it; mountain = +1 defending behind it. Mirror copies everything point-symmetrically.

Card Glossary

Field Manual

Object

Win battles by capturing the enemy headquarters (attack into it; a tie counts) or by attrition — when a player has no cards left, the side that destroyed more enemy materiel wins (1 VP per infantry, 2 per cavalry, 3 per artillery; tie goes to whoever moved second). First to 3 battle victories wins the campaign.

Your Turn

Draw to a hand of 4 (your first turn includes the guaranteed Deploy Infantry card). Play exactly one card and resolve it; the played card is removed from the game, the rest are discarded and recycled. You have only 16 plays per battle — spend them well.

Combat

Attacker: unit's attack + support of your other units adjacent to the defender's hex (+1 if your HQ is adjacent) + 1 if the attacker's own hex has a forest on the crossed edge + card modifiers.
Defender: unit's defense + support of their units adjacent (+1 for adjacent HQ) + 1 if the defender's hex has a mountain on the crossed edge + 1 for a trench in the defender's hex covering that edge.
Higher total wins; loser is destroyed. Winner-attacker advances into the hex. Tie: both destroyed.

Terrain & Buildings

Terrain belongs to the hex it sits in (drawn inset inside it). ■ Forest: +1 attack when its hex's occupant attacks out across a covered edge. ■ Mountain: +1 defense when its hex is attacked across a covered edge. Neither works in the other direction. Trench: +1 defense across its two covered edges, helps any defender, never an attacker; a hex side can't hold both terrain and a trench. HQ: +1 support to all adjacent hexes; units may move, swap, and attack through an HQ hex.

Control

You control the hexes your units and HQ occupy. Deployments must be adjacent to a controlled hex (Airdrop excepted). Trenches are built on a controlled hex.
