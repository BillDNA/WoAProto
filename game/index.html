Theatres of War

Tick the maps you want in the draw pile. New campaigns shuffle only the maps in play.

Cartographer's Table

Terrain belongs to a hex: click just inside a hex's border to cycle forest → mountain → river → empty on that hex's side. Forest = +1 attacking out across it; mountain = +1 defending behind it; river = you can't deploy across it (support still crosses freely). Mirror copies everything point-symmetrically. Board hexes carves the outline itself: click a dashed hex to add it, a solid one to remove it (24-hex ceiling).

Quartermaster's Ledger — Deck Editor

The deck is data (content/decks/*.js). 16 copies per deck, exactly one starting card (guaranteed in the opening hand; count 1). Five deck slots live in this browser — pick one to edit, name it, and mark the ★ one you want the game to use. Tick a card off to bench it. Pick a card on the left to edit it on the right; build its steps with the dropdowns. Card art is looked up by id (art/<id>.jpg) — a new id simply renders as a clean text card.

Changing cards is a rules change in effect — run the Balance Dashboard after, and node game/test.js if you can. LAN play needs the same deck on both devices (share custom-deck.js). CLI tools (balance.js, test.js) read the active deck in content/decks/ — the applied deck here is browser-only.

The War Office — Balance Dashboard

The full balance.js report, in the browser: AI-vs-AI battles across the roster, every metric laid out. Same aggregation as the terminal — the numbers can't disagree.

Field Manual

Object

Win battles by capturing the enemy headquarters (attack into it; a tie counts) or by attrition — when a player has no cards left, the side with more surviving units on the board wins (1 VP per infantry, 2 per cavalry, 3 per artillery; reserves left at home count for nothing; tie goes to whoever moved second). First to 3 battle victories wins the campaign.

Your Turn

Draw to a hand of 4 (your first turn includes the guaranteed Deploy Infantry card). Play exactly one card and resolve it; the played card is removed from the game, the rest are discarded and recycled. You have only 16 plays per battle — spend them well.

Combat

Attacker: unit's attack + support of your other units adjacent to the defender's hex (+1 if your HQ is adjacent) + 1 if the attacker's own hex has a forest on the crossed edge + card modifiers. Attacker support does not arrive across a trenched border (a river no longer blocks support).
Defender: unit's defense + support of their units adjacent (+1 for adjacent HQ) + 1 if the defender's hex has a mountain on the crossed edge. Defender support is never blocked.
Higher total wins; loser is destroyed. Winner-attacker advances into the hex. Tie: both destroyed.

Terrain & Buildings

Terrain belongs to the hex it sits in (drawn inset inside it). ■ Forest: +1 attack when its hex's occupant attacks out across a covered edge. ■ Mountain: +1 defense when its hex is attacked across a covered edge. Neither works in the other direction. ■ River: you can't deploy across it — control doesn't extend over the water. Support, attacks and movement all cross freely, and a river can't be barraged. Trench: the enemy's attacking support may not cross its two covered edges (whoever dug it — a captured trench serves its new master); it adds no defense, never blocks the attack itself, and never hinders the defender. A hex side can't hold both terrain and a trench. HQ: +1 support to all adjacent hexes; units may move, swap, and attack through an HQ hex.

Control

You control the hexes your units and HQ occupy. Deployments must be adjacent to a controlled hex (Airdrop excepted). Trenches are built on a controlled hex.
