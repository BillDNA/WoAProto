## Felt-notes

### red (haiku)

**STRONG:** Cavalry dominated—that Reckless Maneuvers strike at D3 was the pivotal swing. Airdrop's deep reach (D2) forced Blue to respond. HQ adjacency made close combat tense and rewarding.

**WEAK:** Early Infantry felt glass-cannons—T1 and T3 deploys died immediately. Artillery was a trap (0 defense, single-turn lifespan). Trenches helped defensively but didn't swing fights decisively.

**LUCK-DRIVEN:** Blue's turn-2 Conscription (two free units) decided the early game; Red spent 6 turns just catching up. Draw variance matters too much—Red got defensive plays when needing tempo.

**ONE CHANGE:** Allow **one basic Reposition per turn without card play** (like you can resolve any card as it). This guarantees every turn has a meaningful decision even on dead-draw turns and lets players contest tempo instead of folding to Conscription luck. Keeps the card economy tense while reducing "forced skip" turns.

### blue (haiku)

**STRONG:** Early Infantry spam (Conscription) built board presence. Through-HQ attacks opened tactical windows. Artillery deployment showed defensive intent.

**WEAK:** Infantry (def 1) got shredded by Red Cavalry (attack 3) with no answer. Artillery died instantly when deployed (T9). Blue played defensively (Deploy, Deploy, Deploy) and never generated threatening offense—Red dictated tempo.

**LUCK-DRIVEN:** Red's two Cavalry deployments (T5) were hard-to-counter threats. Blue's hand tilted defensive (lots of Deploy cards), forcing reactive play.

**CHANGE:** Raise Infantry **support from 1 → 2**. Right now Infantry stacks don't create enough defensive density to survive Cavalry rush. At support 2, a trio of Infantry supporting each other becomes a real wall, making Blue's "build and hold" viable. Red's Cavalry would still break through, but Blue would trade better and generate the offense window to punish.
