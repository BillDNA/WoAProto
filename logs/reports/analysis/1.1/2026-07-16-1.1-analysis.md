## 4. How it felt

- Both LLM sides independently converged on the **same complaint**: whoever holds Infantry/Cavalry
  reserves and deploys them for a late VP spike (turn 15+) wins the attrition race. Red's proposed
  fix (nerf Artillery support) and Blue's (a deployment cap / "entrenchment tax") point at the same
  felt pattern from opposite sides.
- **Cross-check against the data, per the skill's own rule (felt-notes are impression, not
  ground truth):** this is the exact instrument `dynamic-scrum/docs/data-and-reports.md` names for
  it — reserve-held-at-end. The hard-AI fleet reads **red 10% / blue 9%** reserves at end (normal:
  14%/14%) — i.e. the hard/normal AI populations are *not* meaningfully hoarding; most of the roster
  gets fielded. The felt-note is a 3-game haiku-population anecdote (n=3) that doesn't match the
  much larger AI-population aggregate. **Not promoted to a suggestion** — flag as a watch item (§ 6)
  pending more LLM-population battles, since haiku's play pattern may genuinely differ from the
  hard/normal heuristic (the rubric already notes LLMs play the deck differently — e.g. AvgSeen
  1.8–3.1 vs the hard AI's 4–6 on hoarded cards).
- Both sides felt battles were **close and grinding rather than decided early** ("no early luck
  decided this," "I fell behind in every game... but recovered") — consistent with the balance
  report's healthy Swings and low zero-kill%.
