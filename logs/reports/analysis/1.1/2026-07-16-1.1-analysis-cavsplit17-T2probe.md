## 1. Step-budget interplay (the constraint the ticket flagged)

The rubric hard floor is `printed deploy steps ≥ stock`; the WOA-017 test (`game/test.js`) enforces
the other side, `printed ≤ stock`. Together they pin **printed == stock**. Measured with the
candidate loaded active in-process:

| Unit | Candidate printed deploy steps | Stock (`PIECE_TOTALS`) | WOA-017 (`≤`) | Floor (`≥`) |
|---|--:|--:|:--:|:--:|
| infantry | 7 | 7 | PASS | ok |
| cavalry | 2 | 2 | PASS | ok |
| artillery | 1 | 1 | PASS | ok |
| trench | 3 | 3 | (n/a) | ok |

**Finding: the 17-card breach does NOT cascade into a step-budget test breach.** `cavsplit17-raid-paid`
is *engineered* to keep the deploy budget legal — Raiding Party's extra infantry step is "paid for"
(hence `-raid-paid`) by cutting Airdrop + Ordered Withdraw, landing infantry back at exactly 7. So if
this deck were flipped `active`, `node game/test.js` would stay **237/237 green** on the step-budget
test. Only the **structural 16-card ceiling** is relaxed here; the **deploy-step hard floor is
untouched**. (This probe was run via `dev/balance-report.js --deck`, which never touches the active
flag — the shipped state stayed `default` active throughout.)

## 3. Read — does it beat baseline convincingly?

**Yes, on the `improve-balance-numbers` axis, and it breaks nothing.** Measured wins:
- **Swings +25–30%** on both tiers (2.7→3.5 hard, 2.9→3.6 normal) — the headline. **Inference (not
  proven):** part of this is a battle-length artifact (+3 turns → more room for lead changes). But
  **Drag stays flat** (2.1→2.0, 1.8→1.7), so the extra length is *live back-and-forth, not dead
  circling* — which is exactly the goal's "distribute turns … more back and forth, not just attrition
  and quick rushes." The clearest single-map illustration: **Long March Drag 3.1→2.0** with Swings
  3.4→4.1 (hard) — the fleet's draggiest map got less draggy AND swingier.
- **Fielded 90→94 / 86→90** and **reserves-at-end 10-9→6-6 / 14-14→10-10** — length-independent, genuine
  reduction in hoarding/turtling (the Deploy-Cavalry split + Raiding Party pull more pieces onto the board).
- **First mover 45→47 (hard) / 48→45 (normal)** — both in band. Note this **clears the exact band edge
  that got the earlier `cavsplit-17` rejected in loop-v2 iter2** (that deck put first-mover 3 pts under
  the band; the raid-paid variant does not). The fresh Core Six read supersedes that iter2 rejection.

Measured costs:
- **+3 turns** (hard 28.7→31.9, normal 27.4→30.2). A 17-card deck raises the play cap 32→34, so 31–33
  turns is still under the (new) cap; but several maps now run at/over the old 32 mark (Causeway 32.9,
  Long March 33.0). Pacing is longer, not broken.
- **Normal 0kill 1→4%** — in band (≤5) but a +3 move that sits close to the hard-floor ceiling. **This
  is the one metric worth a confirmation run** before relaxing a documented guardrail: 0kill is a hard
  floor, and a worse draw at higher n could nudge it over. (Hard-tier 0kill is a comfortable 2%.)
- **HQ 23→17 (hard)**, **control 93→86 (normal)** — both still in band; no action implied.

## 4. Recommendation (held for Bill — this is NOT the runner's call)

**Measured. Recommendation: ADOPT (relax the 16-card ceiling to 17 for `cavsplit17-raid-paid`), gated
on one confirmation run. Decision: BILL'S CALL (pending).**

- **Why adopt** — the candidate buys a material, goal-aligned gain (Swings +25–30%, less turtling,
  more units fielded, flatter Drag) for **no broken north star and no breached hard floor on either
  tier**, and it keeps the deploy-step budget legal (7/2/1 exactly → WOA-017 stays green). Under the
  §Search-side **re-measure-to-ship** clause, a T2 escape candidate ships only after a T0/T1 re-measure
  on the standard setup — and this measurement *is* that setup (hard n=60 + normal n=40, Core Six), at
  which the candidate already reads **T0-clean**. The policy clause invoked: §Search-side item 1 (the
  16-card ceiling is the ranked-first dial-able guardrail) + re-measure-to-ship (satisfied here).
- **The gate** — before flipping the guardrail, one **n≥100 normal-tier** confirmation to de-risk the
  0kill drift (1→4%, the only reading near a hard floor). Cheap, and it is the honest application of
  "re-measure before you ship" to the single soft number.
- **On adopt, the constraint change is recorded, not silent** — per [[Goals]] physical-limitations and
  §Search-side re-measure-to-ship, adopting makes **17 the new documented ceiling for this deck**
  (recorded in the rubric + Goals atomically with the flip). The 16-card ceiling remains the default
  guardrail for every other deck.
- **Named third option, if Bill prefers to hold the line:** REJECT — keep the 16-card ceiling as a
  hard physical-board guardrail on principle (systems-explosion control, `physical-limitations`), and
  keep `cavsplit17-raid-paid` as a measured variant on disk. The numbers do not *force* a relax; they
  show the ceiling has a real, now-quantified opportunity cost (a ~25–30% Swings gain left on the
  table). That trade — a physical-design guardrail vs a balance-numbers gain — is precisely the
  `physical-limitations` ↔ `improve-balance-numbers` tension the policy exists to make explicit, and
  it is Bill's to resolve.
