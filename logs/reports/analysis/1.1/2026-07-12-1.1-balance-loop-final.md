# Balance loop v2 — final report (rules 1.1)

_Run 2026-07-12, between sprints. **Cards as the only variable knob** — maps (`core7`), rules (1.1), unit
values and AI weights frozen throughout, so every row below is apples-to-apples. 3 iterations · 4 deck
slots · **2,800 battles** (7 maps × 100, hard-vs-hard, `--once` fixed seed schedule) · 1 seed-1001
LLM feels-match. Noise band ±100/√700 ≈ **±3.8 pts** fleet-wide._

**Bottom line for Bill:** the loop did not ship a card. It found that **the instrument we design cards
with is partly measuring the AI**, that **the first-mover metric is driven by decisiveness rather than
tempo**, and that **the 16-card ceiling — not the cards — is what makes the one good change expensive.**
The three ranked suggestion lists are at the end; §5 (AI levers) is where the leverage is.

## 3. The story

**Iteration 1 — the game is healthy; the deck is what needs work.** All ten north stars pass on
`default`. As a side effect, 700 battles independently re-validated the rules-1.1 guardrails to within
noise (they had been measured at n60). *Before* this could be trusted, the loop had to fix the ruler: the
"baselines to protect" were still 0.x-era, and **attacks/swaps had inverted between eras** (V0 4.9/6.5 →
1.1 6.1/5.7) — grading a fresh run against them would have flagged healthy decks as regressions.

**Then the loop turned on its own instrument.** The iteration-1 read produced a confident card
diagnosis — an "empty middle band", a "deadest card" (Attack +1, AvgSeen 10.85), a 3-for-3 replacement
batch. Four adversarial checkers took it apart, and **the diagnosis was an artifact**:

- **`1stSight%` ≈ the AI's immediate eval delta.** `unitOnBoard: 22` vs `unitReserve: 16` means every
  deploy step crosses a free 6-point gap × the unit's value. That single number rank-orders the entire
  deploy family (cav ×2 = +48 → 79%; art = +30 → 69%; inf = +18 → 18%). `aiPlanTurn` has **no hand
  lookahead** — it cannot hold a card for a better moment, only fail to pick it.
- **`AvgSeen` ≈ `CARD_KEEP`.** The "deep hoard" tail is exactly the two highest burn-reluctance values
  (`mass_assault: 9` → 7.07, `attack_plus1: 8` → 10.85). **Attack +1 is not dead — it is precious.**
  `+1` is the deck's only button that turns a mutual-destruction tie into a clean kill, and the AI hoards
  it for exactly that. The proposed batch would have cut both copies and booked a `−1` attack as
  replacement "kill supply" — an attack the AI declines as −EV. That breaks Tie%, the one guardrail the
  batch existed to protect.
- **The batch was killed before it shipped.** Its funding cut (Conscription) stranded 2 infantry — there
  is **no deploy fallback** in the house rules — sending Drag 2.3 → **3.6** and attacks −20%. It made
  every metric it targeted worse.

**Iteration 2 — the one change that survived, and the ceiling that taxes it.** Splitting Deploy Cavalry
converts a reflex into a real decision (**1stSight 79 → 22**, AvgSeen 1.26 → 2.83) and it is *free*:
cavalry has `sup: 0`, so two cavalry never supported each other anyway — the double-deploy was tempo, not
combined arms. At **17 cards** it improves seven metrics. It only costs something when a 16th-card cut
has to fund it. **The 16-card ceiling — a physical-board guardrail, not a code constraint — is the thing
under test here, not the card.**

**But auto-play cannot be fixed by card design — it relocates.** The moment cavalry vacated the reflex
slot, **Deploy Artillery inherited it: 69% → 77% 1stSight.** The reflex band belongs to whoever holds the
top eval delta. This is an **AI lever**, not a card lever, and no card batch will ever win it.

**Iteration 3 — the tempo hypothesis, rejected, and the structure it exposed.** The split cost 4 points
of first-mover, so iteration 3 tried to buy them back by making Conscription (+36 eval) the guaranteed
opener instead of Deploy Infantry (+18). It made things **worse** (first-mover 40, HQ at the 10% floor).
Across all four decks the relationship is monotonic:

| | turns | HQ% | first-mover |
|---|--:|--:|--:|
| `default` | 28.8 | 22 | **46** |
| `cavsplit-17` | 31.9 | 17 | **42** |
| `cavsplit-16` | 29.9 | 17 | **42** |
| `cavsplit17-tempo` | 33.7 | 10 | **40** |

> **First-mover% is not a tempo metric — it tracks decisiveness.** More deploy tempo → more bodies →
> longer grind → fewer HQ captures → more battles settled by attrition → and attrition ties go to the
> second player. **You raise first-mover% by raising HQ%, not by front-loading the opening.** This also
> explains the whole arc: the cavalry double-deploy was propping up the first mover by enabling a fast
> HQ push, and halving it surfaced an underlying second-mover advantage that was always there.

**None to `default` — by design.** `cavsplit-17` is the only adopt-worthy slot and it needs Bill's

## 5. Suggestions — ranked, all filed for Bill

### 5a. AI levers to add or drop ← **the leverage is here**

1. **The reflex band is an eval-function artifact — decide whether to fix it in the AI.** `unitOnBoard`
   (22) vs `unitReserve` (16) hands every deploy step a guaranteed +6×unitVal, so deploy cards are always
   played on sight and action cards are always hoarded. That, not card design, is why the deck has no
   middle band. Narrowing the gap (e.g. `unitReserve` 16 → 19–20) should compress 1stSight across the
   deploy family. **Verification recipe:** it must clear the WOA-012 beat-hard matchup gate before
   adoption — and note the hazard, tuning the AI to flatter the balance metrics *moves the ruler, not the
   game*. Type: **AI**.
2. **Give the AI hand lookahead, or stop reading its 1stSight/AvgSeen as design signal.** `aiPlanTurn`
   scores only the immediate turn; it *cannot* hold a card for a moment. Every "hoarding" number is
   burn-reluctance, not planning. Cheapest honest option is the second half: retire the metrics as targets
   (done — see the rubric warning block). Type: **AI**.
3. **`CARD_KEEP` is doing more balance work than anyone intended** — it alone separates the hoard band
   (keep 3–5 → AvgSeen 3.8–5.7) from the deep-hoard tail (keep 8–9 → AvgSeen 7–11). It is hand-authored
   and unexamined. Worth an explicit pass. Type: **AI**.

### 5b. Suggested RULE changes

1. **Raise HQ-capture pressure to fix first-mover (§3).** First-mover% tracks HQ% monotonically, and the
   only lever that moved it was decisiveness. Cards barely touch HQ% (it is mostly map geometry — Long
   March sits at HQ 3%). This needs a rules or map answer, not a card one. Expected direction: HQ% ↑ →
   first-mover ↑ toward the 45–55 band. Type: **rules** (spec + `test.js` + `Engine.VERSION` bump).
2. **The `starting: true` card is a live balance lever nobody was treating as one.** Swapping it (Deploy
   Infantry → Conscription) moved first-mover 42 → 40, HQ 17 → 10, and battle length +1.8 turns — from a
   *one-flag* change. It deserves to be a deliberate tuning knob with its own line in the rubric, not an
   incidental deck property. Type: **rules/data**.
3. **Reconsider the 16-card ceiling.** It is a physical-board guardrail. Measured, it is the sole reason
   the cavalry split costs anything: clean at 17, expensive at 16. If the Steam target is a roguelite
   deck-builder with a card pool larger than the deck, this constraint is going to be renegotiated anyway
   — this is the first hard number on what it costs. Type: **design decision (Bill)**.

### 5c. Stats to gather or drop

1. **DROP per-card `Win%` from the printed card table.** Confirmed dead at n=700 — all 13 cards read
   49–52 against a rubric threshold of ±8. It invites exactly the wrong grading instinct. Keep it in the DB.
2. **DEMOTE `1stSight%` and `AvgSeen` from design targets to AI diagnostics** (done in the rubric, §Card
   rubric warning block). They are readouts of `evalState` and `CARD_KEEP`. Still useful — as instruments
   pointed at the AI.
3. **GATHER: an `HQ% × first-mover%` view.** The single most load-bearing relationship this loop found,
   and it took four deck slots to see because nothing puts the two columns side by side.
4. **GATHER: a deploy-step-budget vs stock check** in `test.js` or the report header. The batch's fatal
   flaw (stranded units, no deploy fallback) was invisible until simulated; it is a one-line assertion.

## 6. Measurements audit

- **2,800 battles** across 4 slots, hard-vs-hard, `--once` fixed seeds, `core7`, rules 1.1 — every slot
  step-budget-verified identical to baseline before running, so no comparison is confounded by budget.
- **Every claim above is a ≥700-battle number** (±3.8 pts) except the checker's exploratory 280-battle
  runs, which were **re-measured at n=700 and held** (cavalry split 79→21 ≈ 79→22; Forced March swap
  collapse 2.7 ≈ 2.7).
- **Not measured:** skill premium (hard>normal 60% / hard>easy 78%) has still never been re-run under
  1.1. Nothing in this report leans on it.
- **Confound to respect in any follow-up:** cutting a high-eval deploy card raises every *surviving*
  deploy card's 1stSight through crowd-out alone. Do not read a 1stSight delta as a card's own doing.

## 7. Watch-list

- **First-mover 42% on the adopt-worthy deck** — 3 points under band, reproducible across two fundings.
  This is the trade Bill has to rule on, and §5b.1 says the real fix is elsewhere.
- **Deploy Artillery at 77% 1stSight** on `cavsplit-17` — the relocated watchlist hit. Expect it to move
  again wherever the top eval delta goes.
- **Causeway Red 57% / Saber Ridge Red 57%** on baseline — per-map n=100 is ±10, so not yet real. Both
  moved with the deck slot; likely deck-relative, not map facts.
