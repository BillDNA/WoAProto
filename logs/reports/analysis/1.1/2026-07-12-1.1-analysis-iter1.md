## 4. The card read — the actual finding

**Win% is a dead column.** All 11 cards sit 49–52. The rubric says only deviations beyond ±8 at n≥60
mean anything; at n=700 the whole deck is inside noise. Nothing to learn here, ever, in an attrition
game where both sides play nearly everything. *(→ § 6, a "stats to drop" candidate.)*

**No card fails the deck-budget test.** Noop% is 0 across the board — printed deploy steps are inside
reserve stock. The iter2-era dead-tail (Deploy Infantry 28% noop) has not returned.

The deck sorts into **three families with an empty middle** (all 13 cards, full table):

| Family | Cards | 1stSight% | AvgSeen | What it means |
|---|---|--:|--:|---|
| **Reflex** | Deploy Cavalry (79), Deploy Artillery (69), Conscription (66) | 66–79 | 1.26–1.45 | played on sight — no decision |
| *(gap)* | — | **34–65** | **~2–3** | **nothing lives here** |
| *(edge)* | Airdrop (33), Deploy Infantry (18) | 18–33 | 2.52–2.90 | the only cards even near the middle |
| **Hoard** | Entrench (13), Reckless (12), Naval (11), Careful (7), Ordered Withdraw (7), Forced March (7) | 7–13 | 3.84–5.67 | held for a moment |
| **Deep hoard** | **Mass Assault (4)**, **Attack +1 (1)** | 1–4 | **7.07–10.85** | refused until forced |

**The defect, stated precisely: the deck offers reflexes or it offers hoards. There is no card whose
*timing* is a live tension** — "do I spend this now or hold it one more turn?" That is card-rubric north
star #1 ("adds a decision") failing not per-card but **as a deck shape**.

**And the structural reason is now clear.** Look at what separates the two families: *every* reflex card
is a **deploy** card and *every* hoard card is an **action** card. That is not a coincidence —

> **Deploy steps are the only thing in the card vocabulary backed by a finite, depleting resource
> (unit stock). Attacks never expire, so waiting to play one is free.** A deploy card decays in value if
> you hold it (the body isn't earning attrition VP); an attack card does not. The deck therefore *cannot*
> produce a middle-band card, because no card mixes the two.

There is no `deploy + attack` card in the deck. Every card is pure-deploy or pure-action. **A hybrid sits
in the middle band by construction** — that is the batch's central design lever (§ 5).

### Two cards I initially misread — correcting on the record

- **Ordered Withdraw is NOT dead weight** (my first read, from a truncated table). Simple% 30 is inside
  the rubric's *good* band (<40), it carries the known `CARD_KEEP` burn bias, and AvgSeen 5.67 sits at
  the top of — not outside — the **measured hard-AI family norm of 4–6 for all action cards**. Not a cut.
- **The deep-hoard tail is Attack +1 and Mass Assault**, both of which sit *outside* that 4–6 norm.
  **Attack +1 (AvgSeen 10.85, 1stSight 1%, Simple 26%) is the deck's deadest card by a wide margin** —
  the AI refuses it for eleven hand-appearances, then burns it as a basic attack a quarter of the time.
- **Do not cut hoarded action cards to "fix" drag.** Measured, July 2026: deleting them raised
  tie-decided **11→15→20%** and *produced* the swap-dance signature. Tie is currently 10% and passing —
  the obvious-looking fix actively breaks a green guardrail. Any attack step the batch removes must be
  **put back**. (§ 5 holds printed attack steps at 8, exactly the current count.)

### The hard set-level constraint: every deploy type is EXACTLY at stock

| Type | Printed deploy steps in deck | Reserve stock | Headroom |
|---|--:|--:|--:|
| Infantry | 1 (Deploy Inf) + 3 (Entrench) + 1 (Airdrop) + 2 (Conscription) = **7** | 7 | **0** |
| Cavalry | 2 (Deploy Cavalry) = **2** | 2 | **0** |
| Artillery | 1 = **1** | 1 | **0** |
| Trenches | 3 (Entrench) = **3** | 3 | **0** |

This is *why* Noop% is 0 — and it means **the batch has zero deploy headroom**. Any deploy step added
must be paid for by one cut, or the iter2 dead-tail returns (9 infantry steps vs 7 stock → Deploy
Infantry 28% / Airdrop 26% Noop). This constraint, not card power, is what shapes § 5.

### The one rubric watchlist hit

**Deploy Cavalry — 1stSight 79% at AvgSeen 1.26.** The rubric's auto-play trigger is *>70 with AvgSeen
near 1*; the only card in the deck that hits it. Note it deploys **two** cavalry — and cavalry stock is
**2** — so this single card *is* the entire cavalry reserve, spent in one reflex. WOA-014 pre-seeded
"split Deploy Cavalry" as the first-batch candidate before the data was in; the data agrees.

## 5. Suggestions for the 3-for-3 batch (ranked) — all **data** type (`create-card`)

The batch should target the deck's *shape*, not chase Win%. Three slots, three distinct jobs:

1. **Split Deploy Cavalry into a timing decision** (rubric 4 — the only watchlist hit; pre-seeded by
   WOA-014). Evidence: 1stSight 79% / AvgSeen 1.26, n=1332 plays. Goal: move it into the 30–50% /
   2–3 band — make holding it sometimes right. Should move: 1stSight%, AvgSeen.
2. **Fill the empty middle band with a live-tension card** (rubric 1 — the deck-shape defect). Evidence:
   the 1stSight gap between 33% (Airdrop) and 66% (Conscription) is *unoccupied*. Goal: a card you can
   name the moment for, that costs you to play early. Should move: the deck's decision quality — and it
   is the one suggestion the feels-match (§ 3) is best placed to validate.
3. **Late-game kill supply, to break the swap-dance** (north star 3 / Bill's `improve-balance-numbers`).
   Evidence: The Void Atk 4.5 / Swp 7.7 / Drag 3.7; The Narrows 5.7 / 7.2 / 3.3 — against a fleet mean
   of 6.1 attacks and a 2.5 Drag ceiling. Goal: convert late-game shuffling into contact **by adding
   pressure, not by deleting hoard** (see § 4). Should move: Drag ↓, Atk ↑ on The Void / The Narrows,
   with **Tie% held at ≤15** as the regression gate.

**Measure before/after with the same recipe** (`balance-report 100 hard hard --once --parallel --mapset
core7`) — fixed seeds make iterations 1/2/3 directly comparable.

## 6. Stats to gather or drop (feeds the loop's final report)

- **Drop per-card Win% from the printed card table.** Confirmed dead at n=700 (49–52 across 11 cards,
  rubric threshold ±8). It invites exactly the wrong grading instinct. Keep it in the DB; stop printing it.
- **Gather: a per-card "held-turns" or timing histogram.** AvgSeen is a mean, and the whole finding in
  § 4 is about a *distribution* (two clusters, empty middle) that the mean hides. This loop found the
  gap by eyeballing 11 rows; that will not scale to a 40-card pool.
- The turn-bucketed attack histogram v1 kept flagging would directly diagnose Drag on The Void.

## 7. Watch-list (not suggestions — under-powered at this n)

- **Causeway Red 57% / Saber Ridge Red 57%** — both outside the 45–55 band, but per-map n=100 is ±10, so
  57 is not distinguishable from 55. Watch across iterations 2–3; if a card change holds them at 57+, it's
  real. Note both moved *with the deck slot*, so this may be deck-relative rather than a map fact.
- **Drag 2.4 fleet-wide** — one card batch away from breaching 2.5 in either direction. This is the
  metric to watch on the iteration-2 sweep.
- **Skill premium (hard>normal 60% / hard>easy 78%)** — never re-measured under 1.1. Cited nowhere in
  this analysis for that reason. Worth one matchup run before the loop's final report leans on it.

---

**Bottom line.** Every north star passes; the deck is what needs work. Its defect is a *shape* defect —
three reflex cards, six hoarded cards, and an empty middle where the interesting decisions should live —
plus two maps (The Void, The Narrows) whose swap-dance is pushing fleet Drag onto its ceiling. The
3-for-3 batch: split the one auto-play card, occupy the empty band, and add late-game kill supply
without touching the hoard. Tie% ≤15 is the regression gate on all three.
