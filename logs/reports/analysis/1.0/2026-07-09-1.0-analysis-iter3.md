## The win: dead cards are gone

Deploy budget at exactly stock (7 inf / 2 cav / 1 art) → **Noop% 0 for every card at n=1200**.
Airdrop fully healed (AvgSeen 4.34→2.59, Simple 13→2, 1stSight back to 30). Vanguard performs
exactly as designed: 26% 1stSight, 2.59 AvgSeen, 0 noop, 0 Simple — a starting card that never rots.

## The regression: the endgame lost its teeth (root-cause found)

Tie-decided rose monotonically (11→15→20%) and behaviour crossed into the rubric's swap-dance
signature (Atk 4.6, Swp 7.4). Root cause: **attack-step count stayed 8→8→~7 across decks, but the
tempo-stapled attack cards get played EARLY and are spent by the endgame.** The deleted pure-attack
cards (Attack +1 AvgSeen 10.8, Mass Assault 7.2, Ordered Withdraw 5.8) were "hoarded" — which
accidentally made them the deck's late-game kill supply. Deleting the hoard deleted the endgame.
Long games now decay into reposition-shuffles and equal-score deck-outs (Long March Tie 32%,
The Void 28%, Frontier 24%).

Secondary factor: exact-stock deploy budget removed redundancy — fielded share dipped to 87%
(draw-order luck now determines whether the 7th infantry ever lands).

## Measurement lessons this iteration

- **AvgSeen is a two-faced metric**: high AvgSeen = "hoarded/weak" per the rubric, but iteration 3
  proved hoarding can be FUNCTIONAL (late-game supply). The rubric read must pair AvgSeen with
  fleet Tie%/behaviour before condemning a card. Candidate rubric refinement filed for wrap.
- Zero-kill and Tie% are the deck-endgame instruments; they moved together all session.
- Fielded-share (87%) quietly flags deploy-redundancy loss — worth watching at the deck level.
