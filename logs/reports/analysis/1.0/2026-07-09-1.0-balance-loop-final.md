## The story in three sentences

1. **Iteration 1** deleted the three hoarded pure-attack cards and stapled attacks to tempo —
   Simple% collapsed (25–33 → 1–4) and LLM players adopted the new cards on sight, but the swap
   oversubscribed infantry deploys (9 steps vs 7 stock).
2. **Iteration 2's data** exposed that as 26–28% dead cards (caught by the Noop% column this
   session restored); **iteration 3** fixed the budget to exact stock — dead cards went to zero
   fleet-wide — but tie-decided battles climbed monotonically (11→15→20%) with a swap-dance
   signature, revealing that the "hoarded" attack cards had been the deck's late-game kill supply.
3. **bestof** keeps the noop-free chassis AND restores Attack +1 ×2 as the deliberate endgame
   reserve: tie back to 11%, swaps 3.6, attacks 5.7, zero dead cards — the healthiest deck measured,
   with three genuinely new decisions (Vanguard, Over the Top, Creeping Barrage) earning their slots.

## Measurements audit (top-level goal 1)

**Added this session (already shipped):**
- **Noop% column** in the card table (report-model.js + dashboard) — found the iteration-2
  dead-card regression that the old format was blind to. The single most valuable instrument change.
- `--deck` on balance-report (+ parallel workers) and true mapset rotation in claude-plays
  matches — without these the loop's apples-to-apples premise was silently broken.
- Deck/mapset ids in report meta + filenames (self-describing artifacts).

**Discovered measurement rules (now in the rubrics, each with measured evidence):**
- Deck-budget corollary: printed deploy steps vs stock predicts dead cards (9-vs-7 → 26–28% noop).
- Map numbers are deck-relative: Causeway read Red 62 (delete-grade) under iter2 and best-in-fleet
  4.2 under bestof. Never grade a map outside its deck context.
- AvgSeen is two-faced: hoarding can be functional (late-game supply); condemn only alongside
  fleet Tie%/behaviour, and cross-check the LLM population (AI hoards ALL action cards at 4–6;
  LLMs played the same cards at 1.8–3.1).

**Still missing (filed, not built):**
- Per-map control-tracks-win and first-blood columns (data exists in every agg; only the fleet
  line prints — map rubric #5 needs a second filtered run today).
- HQ hex-distance as a printed map property (rubric #4 "count by hand" — one-liner to compute).
- Per-unit deploy/attack/death counts (unit rubric has no instrument at all).
- Attack-play TIMING distribution (early vs late) — this session's central mechanism was invisible
  in aggregates and had to be inferred; a turn-bucketed attack histogram per deck would have shown
  it directly. Best candidate for the next instrument.
- `seriesFlipped` aggregate across matches (game-1 winner took the match 5/6 — is first-to-3
  smoothing anything? n too small to say).

**Weak instruments confirmed:** per-card Win% (hugs 50 by construction — kept as sanity only);
