## The two failures (both measured, both caused by the iter1 swap)

1. **Deploy-step oversubscription.** iter2 prints 9 infantry-deploy steps against the 7-piece
   stock (and 5 trench steps vs 3). Cards whose ONLY step is a deploy die when drawn after the
   stock is spent: Deploy Infantry 28% noop, Airdrop 26%, Entrench 7%. Confirmed in real play —
   Entrench no-oped 7 of 43 LLM plays (16%). The restored Noop% column caught this; the old
   report format would have missed it entirely. → Fix: iteration 3 rebalances the budget to
   exactly stock (rubric's new deck-budget corollary).
2. **Tie-decided battles rose 11→15%**, with 5 of 14 LLM battles ending as 33-turn equal-score
   deck-outs. Maps at/above 16%: Frontier 18, The Weir 18, The Clearing 19, Causeway 16,
   Long March 16. → Iteration 3's two free copy slots target tie-breaking aggression.

## What worked (the tempo-attack hypothesis, half-confirmed)

- **Simple% collapsed**: deleted trio read 25–33% (printed action not worth playing);
  Shock Troops / Storm and Hold / Sappers read 4 / 4 / 1. Printed actions now beat the burn.
- **Noop 0** on all three new cards — the multi-step design risk didn't materialize on them.
- **Population split discovered**: LLMs play the new cards eagerly (Shock Troops 2nd-most-played,
  AvgSeen 3.1; Storm and Hold 1.8 ≈ on sight) while the hard AI hoards all action cards at
  AvgSeen 4–6 (its family norm — deploy-first play style, not per-card weakness). Measurement
  lesson: **hard-AI AvgSeen alone cannot condemn a card; cross-check the LLM population.**
- **Sappers fails in BOTH populations** (AI 5.1 / 1stSight 3; LLM 7.2) — genuinely
  over-situational. Deleted in iteration 3 (its designer's own kill).
